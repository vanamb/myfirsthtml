Welcome to my site!

I am a Master of Public Health candidate in Environmental Health Science and Policy at George Washington University in Washington, D.C. I currently work as a climate assessments intern at the North Carolina Institute for Climate Studies, where I am learning more about figure and graphics development, data visualization tools, metadata, and web design. I am expected to graduate in May 2025 and hope to work with communities in advocating for environmental justice and bridging the gap between data and action.

Before attending graduate school, I worked as an Assistant Language Teacher on the JET Programme in Susaki, Kochi, Japan. During this time, I also served as a Block Representative for Emergency Response and a volunteer firefighter for the town of Susaki. I received my B.A. in Anthropology and Japanese Language and Culture Studies with a minor in International Studies and a certificate in Japanese-English translation from UNC Charlotte in 2021. I am deeply passionate about environmental health, justice, and making science accessible to those who need it most.
